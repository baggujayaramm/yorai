# Data Retention Foundation

Yorai should not retain data indefinitely without a reason. These rules are launch-stage guidance and require legal/operational review before broad public launch.

## Retention Categories

- Accounts and sessions: keep active account records while the account is active; expire/revoke sessions according to configured session lifetime and security events.
- Notifications: keep recent user-facing notification history for product usefulness; review archival/deletion windows before launch.
- Reports, moderation records, warnings, restrictions, and appeals: retain long enough to support safety, repeat-abuse review, and auditability.
- Feedback: retain until triage/resolution and product learning are complete; avoid exposing internal notes to users.
- Waitlist entries and invite records: retain for beta access management and duplicate prevention, then archive or delete when no longer needed.
- Import history and college change records: retain for provenance, rollback review, and data quality audits.
- Operational events and audit logs: retain aggregate/error metadata without passwords, tokens, private content, or secrets.

## Deletion Requests

Account deletion requests enter a cooling-off state. Yorai can hide profile context, revoke sessions, remove private profile information, and preserve public discussion structure with anonymized or minimized identity context where needed for continuity and moderation.

## Review Needed

Define concrete retention windows with legal review before public launch. Do not automatically delete records needed for active moderation, safety, fraud prevention, or legal obligations.
