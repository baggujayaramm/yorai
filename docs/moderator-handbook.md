# Moderator Handbook

Yorai moderation protects useful student context, not attacks. Preserve respectful lived experience where possible while removing private information, harassment, spam, and unsafe uploads.

## Decision Principles

- Prioritize privacy risks, personal attacks, unsupported serious allegations, and safety issues.
- Do not treat criticism as a violation by itself.
- Keep branch-specific and changed-recently context visible when it is respectful and useful.
- Use warnings and temporary restrictions proportionally.
- Escalate repeated abuse, private-data leaks, or suspected coordinated manipulation.

## Privacy Handling

- Hide or redact roll numbers, phone numbers, emails, addresses, QR codes, signatures, ID cards, marksheets, and visible private chat identities.
- Keep context attachments moderator-only until reviewed.
- Do not expose reporter identities publicly.
- Do not share moderator notes outside moderation/admin surfaces.

## Representative Requests

College representatives can request factual metadata corrections only. They cannot remove student experiences, edit discussions, suppress criticism, access reporter identities, access moderator notes, or influence ordering.

## Appeals and Restoration

Review appeals calmly. Restore content when the privacy/safety issue is resolved or when a moderation action was too broad. Keep audit history for accountability.
